KOSGODA SEA TURTLE HATCHERY

  • The Kosgoda Sea Turtle Conservation Association is a non-profit organization dedicated to conserving and protecting sea turtles along the southwestern coast of Sri Lanka. The organization operates a turtle hatchery and offers guided tours and educational programs for visitors.



  • The hatchery was established to combat the declining population of sea turtles in Sri Lanka due to factors such as habitat destruction, poaching, and accidental capture in fishing nets. It works closely with local communities, fishermen, and government authorities to raise awareness and implement conservation measures.



    • Read More
  • At the Kosgoda Sea Turtle Hatchery, visitors can learn about the different species of sea turtles found in Sri Lanka, including the Green Turtle, Loggerhead Turtle, Olive Ridley Turtle, Hawksbill Turtle, and Leatherback Turtle. The hatchery collects eggs that have been laid on the beach and protects them in a safe environment until they hatch. This helps to prevent predators and human disturbances from endangering the eggs and hatchlings.




  • Once the hatchlings emerge, the hatchery ensures their safe release into the ocean. They are typically released during the evening or early morning to enhance their chances of survival. Visitors often have the opportunity to witness these releases and learn about the challenges that sea turtles face during their early stages of life.
